Content

Home page

Kraft mac and cheese for dinner, we’ve all been there. It's easy and just right there, but that can’t be the only option for college students that just don’t know how to cook. You can find simple recipes that aren’t difficult to follow for meals that are tasty and quick here. This is specifically for college students that aren’t aware of how to create real food on their own, but it can also apply to anyone that isn’t good at cooking. It has also come to my attention that there is a frightening lack of fruits and vegetables in the meals of college students. Therefore, this covers the basics of how to feed yourself during the day and ensures there are at least some fruits and vegetables in your diet.

Dinners

DINNER Taco soup: Time- 20 minutes, materials - 1 pot

  • 1 large can of petite diced tomatoes (28 oz)
  • 1 can of kidney beans
  • 1 small container of ground beef
  • ½ onion
  • 1 packet of taco seasoning
  • 1 bag of frozen sweet white corn
  • salt and pepper
In a pot, cook the ground beef with the diced ½ onion. Drain the excess fat out of the meat, then cook in the taco seasoning. Cook for about 2 minutes. Add the petite diced tomatoes, kidney beans, and corn. Simmer for 5-10 minutes. Add salt and pepper to your liking. This will make an entire pot full, so it can be saved for leftovers or served to a group.

Spaghetti: Time 20 mins, materials- 2 pots
  • 1 package of Spaghetti noodles
  • 1 jar of Prego tomato sauce
  • 1 small container of ground beef
  • ½ onion
  • Sour cream (optional)
  • Salt and pepper
In one pot, fill with water and sprinkle in some salt. Add spaghetti noodles, however many you want. Boil noodles until al dente. Drain. In the other pot, cook ground beef with ½ onion and salt and pepper until brown. Drain the excess fat from the meat. Add tomato sauce. Add sauce and noodles together. Add sour cream to the sauce to make it creamy (optional).

Lunch/Snack

LUNCH/SNACK Apple dip: Time- 5 minutes, Materials- small bowl

  • 1 cup vanilla yogurt
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1- 2 apples
In a bowl, mix yogurt, peanut butter, and vanilla. Slice the apple. Enjoy.

Black bean salsa: Time- 15 minutes, Materials - cutting board, large bowl, colander
  • 6 Roma tomatoes
  • ¼ onion
  • 1 can of black beans
  • bag frozen sweet white corn
  • bunch of cilantro
  • Salt and pepper
  • tbsp Lime juice
  • Avocado (optional)
In a colander, add the can of black beans and the bag of corn. Rinse black beans and corn under warm water until corn is thawed. Set aside to drain. Dice tomatoes, onion, cilantro, and avocado (optional). Add to the bowl. Add corn and beans to the bowl. Sprinkle salt and pepper. Add 2 tbsp of lime juice. This creates a large bowl, it can be saved for leftovers or served to a group.
